1.    Imagine that you have trained your dog, St. Bernard Bernie, to carry a box of 10 floppy disks (1.44MB capacity each, assume 1024 bytes = 1kb, 1000kb = 1MB) instead of a flask of brandy. The dog can travel to your side, wherever you may be, at a speed of 18 km/hour. For what range of distances does Bernie have a higher data rate than a 28 kbps telephone line?

(kbps = kilobit per second, 1 byte = 8 bit)

 

 

2.    Consider the following scheme used to compress text. The most common 200 words in the text are encoded in 8-bit byte as the numbers 0 through 199. Codes 200 through 249 could be used to indicate that the next byte contained a word code. This system allows the 200 most common words to be encoded in 1 byte, and the next 12,800 in 2 bytes. The remaining six codes, 250 through 255, could be used to indicate that ASCII string follows, with the length in the next byte.

Suppose that a text consists of words (and spaces) only, with the most common 200 words accounting for 75% of all words, and the next 12,800 words accounting for 10% of all words. If the average word has k characters, find the percent savings as a function of k.
